## Forensic Statistics Output

The forensic audit is the more important result for this paper. `paperscope.analysis.forensic_stats` reports:

- **14 failures** classified as impossible
- **7 additional flags** classified as suspicious
- Final verdict: **severe data integrity concerns (21 issues)**

The strongest issues surfaced by the audit:

1. **Impossible BDI mean**
   Mg end mean `11.26` with `n=26` fails GRIM.

2. **Impossible serum magnesium change variance**
   Placebo group pre/post/change SDs imply `r = 1.2664`, which is impossible.

3. **Paired-test p-values badly inconsistent**
   Several reported paired-change p-values are orders of magnitude away from recalculated values.

4. **Table arithmetic failures**
   Multiple reported change scores in Table 2 do not equal end minus baseline.

5. **Impossible negative SDs**
   Several placebo change "SD" values are negative, which cannot be valid standard deviations.

6. **SPRITE failures on BDI summaries**
   Several reported mean/SD combinations could not be generated by any valid integer-valued BDI dataset.

This is not a subtle pattern. Even before interpretive questions about effect size or causality, the summary statistics themselves appear internally inconsistent at multiple independent points.

## Bottom Line

If this were being assessed as a critical read rather than a formal review, the central conclusion is straightforward: **the forensic statistics audit raises severe credibility problems that dominate the case**. The overclaiming signal from the critical-read pass is secondary; the main concern is that the reported summary statistics contain multiple impossible or mutually inconsistent values.

The appropriate next step would be raw-data scrutiny or independent verification from authors/editors, not fine-grained interpretation of the claimed treatment effect.
